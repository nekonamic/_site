Research Direction

Dielectric materials, which can store energy in an electric field, are commonly used in civil engineering for electrical insulation, electromagnetic wave isolation, capacitors, sensors, and more.

Here are several applications of dielectric materials in civil engineering:

Electrical insulation: In power transmission and distribution systems, dielectric materials are often used for the insulation of equipment such as cables, insulators, and transformers. With their high dielectric constant, dielectric materials are excellent electrical insulators, preventing current leakage and arc formation in electrical equipment.

Electromagnetic wave isolation: Dielectric materials can be used to isolate electromagnetic waves and protect electronic devices from interference. In civil engineering, dielectric materials can be used to manufacture electromagnetic shielding materials, for example, to reduce electromagnetic interference on highways or railways.

Capacitors: Dielectric materials are often used to manufacture capacitors, which can store charge and release it when needed. In civil engineering, capacitors are commonly used in power electronics equipment, such as for improving energy quality, stabilizing grid voltage, and more.

Sensors: Dielectric materials can also be used to manufacture sensors that can sense electric or magnetic fields in the surrounding environment and convert this information into electrical signals. In civil engineering, dielectric sensors can be used for monitoring structures, such as deformation and vibration, earthquake warning, non-destructive testing, and more.

In summary, dielectric materials have a wide range of applications in civil engineering, which can improve the efficiency and reliability of power equipment, protect electronic equipment from interference, and achieve monitoring and control of structures.

Ba(Cu1/2W1/2)O3 giant dielectric ceramics are ceramic materials with extremely high dielectric constants and dielectric polarization rates. In civil engineering, giant dielectric ceramics are often used to manufacture sensors, isolation materials, energy storage devices, and other applications.

Sensors: Giant dielectric ceramics can be used to manufacture pressure sensors, acceleration sensors, and other types of sensors. These sensors can detect information such as pressure and acceleration in the surrounding environment and convert it into electrical signals. In civil engineering, these sensors can be used to monitor the vibration, deformation, and earthquake warning of structures.

Isolation materials: Giant dielectric ceramics have high damping performance and strength, which makes them suitable for manufacturing isolation materials. In civil engineering, these isolation materials can be used to reduce the impact of earthquakes or other external forces on structures, thereby improving their stability and safety.

Energy storage devices: Giant dielectric ceramics have high dielectric polarization rates, which make them suitable for manufacturing energy storage devices such as capacitors and supercapacitors. In civil engineering, these energy storage devices can be used to improve energy quality, stabilize grid voltage, and other applications.

Giant dielectric ceramics have a wide range of applications in civil engineering, including improving the stability and safety of structures, monitoring and controlling structures, and improving energy quality. Therefore, we conducted in-depth research on the properties of Ba(Cu1/2W1/2)O3 high-dielectric ceramics to find BWC ceramics with even higher dielectric performance to meet the needs of practical engineering and daily life.

TENG are a novel type of micro-power device that generates electricity through mechanical friction. In civil engineering, TENGs can be applied in the following areas:

Structural health monitoring: TENGs can be utilized to monitor the health of structures by measuring mechanical parameters such as vibrations and strains. By distributing multiple TENGs at different locations on a structure, it is possible to monitor the entire structure and detect damage or fatigue in a timely manner.

Energy harvesting: Many mechanical friction phenomena exist in structures, such as wind blowing or pedestrian and vehicle traffic. By harnessing this mechanical energy, TENGs can be used to recover this energy and reduce energy waste.

Environmental monitoring: By distributing TENGs in soil or water surrounding a structure, it is possible to capture and convert mechanical friction energy in the environment, thereby enabling environmental monitoring and protection.

Additionally, TENG technology can be used in the manufacturing of smart roads and bridges to collect real-time data on traffic conditions, loads, and temperature. This technology is of great significance for the development of smart cities. Currently, we are exploring the use of positive and negative pole thin films doped with materials to search for films with higher mechanical energy conversion efficiency.
